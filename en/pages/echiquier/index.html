The Chessboard is neither a game one wins, nor a test, nor a way of ranking oneself or judging others. It is a pedagogical map of the inner path. It shows how a human being can rise, fall, return, go astray, correct himself, receive a help — then be tempted again. A virtue can raise; but if the self appropriates it, it can become a fall. A fall can save, if it provokes a sincere return. The path is not a straight line.

First visit

How to enter without getting lost

Don't look first for “your square”. Enter as into a mirror: a square reveals a movement of the soul, an arrow shows a consequence, a remedy opens a return. The Chessboard is worked slowly.

  1. Choose a door. For a first time, begin with “How I feel” or the first step.
  2. Open a square. Read the Arabic term, its plain sense, then what it shows inwardly.
  3. Spot the movement. An arrow raises; a chain or a grapnel signals a fall or a regression.
  4. Receive a question. Diagnose no one: observe only what resonates in you.
  5. Lay down a note. The journal stays on your device and keeps a trace of the inner work.
