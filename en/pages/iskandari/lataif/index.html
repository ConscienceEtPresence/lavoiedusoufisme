The Shādhilī way

A way marked by sobriety, shukr, adab and service. It does not seek the brilliance of miracles, but the rightness of the heart in ordinary life. It accepts work, family, trade — and sees in them the very ground of transformation.

The transmission

Abū al-Ḥasan al-Shādhilī (d. 656/1258) → Abū al-ʿAbbās al-Mursī (d. 686/1287) → Ibn ʿAṭāʾ Allāh al-Iskandarī (d. 709/1309). Three masters, one silsila, an attentiveness to the baraka that passes through companionship and adab.

A note on the karāmāt

The book recounts extraordinary gifts — visions, unveilings, apparent rescues — yet Ibn ʿAṭāʾ Allāh speaks of them soberly: the karāmāt are not the goal, they are signs left along the way to reassure the heart of the companion. The way remains that of servanthood, not of the extraordinary.
