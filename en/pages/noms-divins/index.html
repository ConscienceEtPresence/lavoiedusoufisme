The 99 Beautiful Names

al-Asmāʾ al-Ḥusnā · the most beautiful Names

A contemplative school in ninety-nine doors — each Name is a face of the Real, and an inward call.

The Asmāʾ al-Ḥusnā are, for the Sufi tradition, so many windows opened onto the ungraspable. No Name exhausts God; each evokes Him under a particular savour — Mercy, Justice, Beauty, Patience, Light, Silence. To contemplate them is to learn to see the world and oneself through these successive clarities.

This module follows, step by step, the commentary of a single master: al-Kāfījī, a 15th-century Ottoman scholar. His method braids three threads — language, reason, the heart — and ends each Name with this fitting question: what is the servant's share?

Filter by family, or let a Name be drawn for you at random, to meditate on today.

Three families, a single Face

The tradition distinguishes the Names of Jamāl (Beauty, gentleness, mercy), the Names of Jalāl (Majesty, power, justice), and the Names of Kamāl (Perfection, Essence — there where Beauty and Majesty coincide). These are not boxes: a single heart passes through these three savours, as a day passes through dawn, noon and dusk.

A single source All the content of this module is drawn from one work: Sharḥ al-Asmāʾ al-Ḥusnā by Muḥyī al-Dīn Muḥammad ibn Sulaymān al-Kāfījī (788–879 H / 1386–1474), an Ottoman scholar born in Crimea, head of the Hanafites in Cairo, master of al-Suyūṭī. The rubrics luġawī / ʿaqlī / ṣūfī and the servant's share faithfully reproduce his structure. Other masters (al-Ghazālī, Ibn ʿArabī, Ibn ʿAṭāʾ Allāh) will come into dialogue in a future stage.
